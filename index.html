The 1st International Workshop · Sustainable & Efficient LVA Models

The 1st International Workshop on Sustainable and Efficient Language, Vision, and Action Models (SELVA)

Confronting the computational costs of scaling Language, Vision, and Action Models.

ACL 2026 Co-located Workshop San Diego, California, USA Tentative: one-day hybrid workshop

Overview

SELVA focuses on efficiency and sustainability across language, vision, and action models, especially for real-time and resource-constrained deployment.

Recent advances in LLMs, VLMs, and VLA systems are accompanied by rapidly increasing compute and energy costs. SELVA aims to foster cross-disciplinary discussion spanning NLP, computer vision, robotics, and systems to develop methods that are both high-performing and computationally feasible.

Efficient Architectures for Multimodality
MoE, state-space models, and sparse routing for language-vision-action fusion.
Model Compression and Acceleration
Sub-4-bit quantization, structured pruning, and distillation.
Sustainable Training and Real-Time Inference
Energy-aware pretraining, PEFT, and optimized engines for low latency.
Efficiency for Embodied AI
Efficient world models, policy optimization, and low-compute interaction loops.
Metrics, Measurement, and Societal Impact
Transparent energy/latency reporting and broader environmental implications.

Call for Papers

We welcome high-quality original research and system papers related to efficient and sustainable LVA models.

Submission Scope

  • Efficient multimodal and embodied AI architectures
  • Compression, quantization, pruning, and distillation for foundation models
  • Systems optimization and low-latency deployment pipelines
  • Energy/latency benchmarks and trustworthy reporting
  • Sustainable AI policy and broader impact analyses

Hybrid format planned: invited talks, contributed talks, poster session, and award ceremony.
